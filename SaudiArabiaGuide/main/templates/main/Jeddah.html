Historical Places

Al-Balad

Al-Balad, the historic heart of Jeddah, stands as a living testament to the city's rich past and vibrant culture. Wandering through its narrow alleys, visitors encounter beautifully preserved coral-stone buildings adorned with intricately carved wooden windows, showcasing the traditional architecture of the region. The bustling Souk Al-Alawi adds to the allure, offering a sensory journey with its vibrant market stalls, aromatic spices, and handmade crafts. As the sun sets, the historic district comes to life with the echoes of muezzins' calls to prayer and the warm glow of traditional lanterns, creating an enchanting ambiance that captures the essence of Jeddah's timeless charm.

Art Galleries

Jeddah's art scene flourishes with a variety of galleries, creating a vibrant cultural tapestry. From contemporary showcases to traditional Saudi art, these spaces celebrate diverse artistic expressions. Nestled in Al-Balad and modern districts alike, Jeddah's galleries offer a captivating glimpse into the city's dynamic and ever-evolving art landscape.

Fakieh Aquarium

a mesmerizing aquatic attraction that invites visitors into the enchanting world beneath the waves. With a diverse collection of marine life, including vibrant coral reefs and exotic sea creatures, the aquarium provides an immersive experience for all ages. From educational exhibits to thrilling underwater tunnels, Fakieh Aquarium offers a captivating journey into the wonders of the ocean, making it a must-visit destination for marine enthusiasts and families seeking a memorable aquatic adventure in Jeddah.
